  • Cloud Computing

    Cloud computing means you are doing your computing on somebody else's computer. Looking ahead a little, cloud - previously called grid computing - will become very widespread. It's much cheaper than buying your own computing infrastructure, or maybe you don't have the power to do what you want on your own computer.

  • Entrepreneurship

  • Information Security

    Information security, sometimes shortened to InfoSec, is the practice of defending information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, perusal, inspection, recording or destruction. It is a general term that can be used regardless of the form the data may take (e.g. electronic, physical).

  • Web Development

    Come and learn about different platforms and methods of designing your website.
    If there's one thing you learn by working on a lot of different Web sites, it's that almost any design idea--no matter how appallingly bad--can be made usable in the right circumstances, with enough effort.

  • Internet of Things(IOT)

    The Internet of Things (IoT) is the network of physical objects—devices, vehicles, buildings and other items embedded with electronics, software, sensors, and network connectivity—that enables these objects to collect and exchange data.

  • Robotics

    Robots are interesting because they exist as a real technology that you can really study- you can get a degree in Robotics- and they also have all this pop- culture real estate that they take up in people's minds.
